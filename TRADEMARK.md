## Permitted uses — no permission needed

You may use the Quell name and marks WITHOUT written permission for:

### 1. Factual reference
Saying that your software "supports Quell", "is compatible with Quell",
"compiles Quell circuits", or "uses the Quell compiler" is factually
accurate and permitted.

```
✓  "This tool accepts Quell (.quell) files as input."
✓  "Supports Quell, Qiskit, OpenQASM, and Cirq formats."
✓  "Built with the open-source Quell compiler from Magnobit."
```

### 2. Educational and research use
Academic papers, tutorials, blog posts, conference talks, and classroom
materials may freely reference and describe Quell.

```
✓  "We implemented our algorithm in Quell and compiled it to Qiskit."
✓  "Quell is an open-source quantum circuit language developed by Magnobit."
```

### 3. Open-source projects
Open-source projects that use or extend Quell may mention this in their
README and documentation, provided they do not imply official endorsement
by Magnobit.

```
✓  "This project uses Quell for circuit representation."
✗  "Official Quell IDE (endorsed by Magnobit)"  ← prohibited
```

### 4. Criticism, commentary, and comparison
You may compare Quell with other tools, write critical reviews, or
discuss trade-offs, as long as comparisons are factually accurate.

## Prohibited uses — permission required

The following uses require **written permission from Magnobit**:

### 1. Product names and brands
You may NOT use "Quell" as part of your product or service name in a way
that could cause confusion about its origin or imply official status.

```
✗  "QuellPro", "Quell Studio", "Quell Cloud"  (product names)
✗  "The Quell Foundation"  (implies official body)
✗  "Quell IDE by [YourCompany]"  (implies official association)
```

### 2. Domain names
Domains containing "quell" paired with quantum computing terms are not
permitted without written permission.

```
✗  quelllang.com, quellqc.io, quell-compiler.dev
✓  my-quantum-tool.dev  (no "quell" in domain)
```

### 3. Forks published under the Quell name
If you fork the Quell compiler and publish it publicly, you must give your
fork a clearly different name. You may NOT publish a fork as "Quell" or
"Quell [version]" even if you make significant modifications.

```
✗  Publishing a fork as "Quell 2.0" or "Quell Extended"
✓  "MyQuantumLang (based on Quell by Magnobit)"
```

### 4. Commercial services that wrap Quell
If you offer a paid service where Quell compilation is a primary advertised
feature, you need a commercial licence from Magnobit. Contact:
partnerships@magnobit.com.

### 5. Logos and visual marks
The ⬡ hexagon logo, the Magnobit wordmark, and any visual adaptations of
Quell branding may not be used in any product, service, or publication
without explicit written permission.

## How to apply for permission

Email **legal@magnobit.com** with:

1. Your name and organisation
2. What mark you want to use and how
3. The product, service, or publication where it will appear
4. A URL or description of the context

We aim to respond within 10 business days. Permission is typically granted
quickly for open-source tools, academic work, and compatible integrations.
